Gold comes in different types based on its purity, colour, etc. If you are wondering how many types of gold in India are available, the answer is quite a few. Let’s start with classification based on purity. The following table will simply explain this:

Gold Type Purity Gold Content Best used For
24K Gold 99.9% Pure gold Investment coins, bars
22K Gold 91.6% Gold + silver/copper Indian jewellery
18K Gold 75% Gold + 25% base metal Modern jewellery
14K Gold 58.5% Mixed with silver/copper/zinc Everyday wear
10K Gold 41.7% Lowest legal gold standard Budget jewellery. Not so common in India

For investment purposes, 24k gold is preferred. For durability and value, 22K gold will be the right choice. 18K gold is perfect for trendy and designer pieces.

Types of Gold by Colour

This might make you wonder. Gold isn’t always yellow. Jewellers create different colours of gold by mixing pure gold with other base metals. Each type has its own beauty and use. For those wondering how many types of gold in India, the breakdown below will clearly explain the types of gold by color.

Yellow Gold

This is the most traditional and widely used type in India. It is made by combining gold with metals such as silver and copper. These metals will give it a rich tone. This is perfect for classic gold jewellery.

White Gold

This is the most traditional and widely used white metals like palladium and nickel.It is usually coated with rhodium to enhance its shine.It will look similar to platinum.this is the perfect choice for a modern style.

Rose Gold

It is also known as pink gold.This is created by mixing gold with copper.it has a soft and romantic blush tone.it is a popular choice for trendy rings and watches.

Green Gold

Green gold is a rare type.this is made by mixing gold with silver or cadminum.it has a natural greenish tint.this is mostly used in artistic or vintage jewellery.

Blue Gold

Blue gold is made by combining gold with indium or gallium.this will give it a subtle bluish shade.it is very rare and usually found in designer pieces.

Purple Gold

It is created by adding aluminium to gold. it looks beautiful but is more fragile.so, it is mostly used in accept pieces or luxury designs.

Black Gold

It is not naturally black.it is made by treating real gold with alloys using surface techniques.it has a bold look and is often used in designer jewellery
